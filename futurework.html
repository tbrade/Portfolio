Future Work

Picture of an African clawed frog © Brian Gratwick retrieved from WSDFW. The name is derived from the claws on the hind feet.

The African clawed frog (Xenopus laevis) is an invasive species. This frog has the potential to eat native amphibians, spread the Bd fungus which has killed many amphibians around the world, and could affect the reproductive rates of native amphibians. In the last decade, the African clawed frog has been found in three cities in Washington State: Bothel, Issaquah and Lacey. An analysis of the potential future distribution of the African clawed frog specifically for Western Washington has not been conducted.

My project aims to create a potential future species range map for the African clawed frog using both MaxEnt and R. The accuracy of the models generated by MaxEnt and R will be determined by AUC and TSS values.

The products of this analysis will be posted to a public website explaining the dangers of the African clawed frog, how to identify the frogs, and where to report sightings.

These results may inform future more detailed analyses of future African clawed frog distribution in Western Washington.
